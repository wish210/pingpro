# Table Tennis Blade Technical Knowledge

## Playing Styles & Blade Categories

### ITTF Classification System
| Code | Style | Speed | Control | Typical Ply |
|------|-------|-------|---------|-------------|
| DEF | Defensive (chop) | Low | Very High | 5W, thick |
| DEF+ | Modern defense | Low-Med | High | 5W |
| ALL | Allround | Medium | High | 5W |
| ALL+ | Allround-offensive | Med-High | Medium-High | 5W or 5W+2C |
| OFF- | Offensive minus | High | Medium | 5W+2C or 7W |
| OFF | Offensive | High | Medium-Low | 5W+2C |
| OFF+ | Very offensive | Very High | Low | 7W+2C or 3W+2C |

### What Players Actually Need
| Player Level | Best Category | Price Tolerance | Key Need |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| Beginner (0–1 year) | ALL to ALL+ | $30–$50 | Forgiveness, control |
| Intermediate (1–3 years) | ALL+ to OFF- | $50–$90 | Balance, developing technique |
| Advanced (3–7 years) | OFF- to OFF | $80–$150 | Speed + enough control |

Most SALES happen in intermediate tier — players upgrading from premade rackets.

## Blade Anatomy

```
Cross-section of a 5W+2C blade:

  ┌─────────────────────┐  ← Face ply (limba/koto) 0.5–0.7mm
  ├─────────────────────┤  ← Carbon layer (ALC/ZLC) 0.2–0.3mm
  ├─────────────────────┤  ← Sub-face (ayous/spruce) 0.8–1.2mm
  ├─────────────────────┤  ← Core (ayous/balsa/kiri) 2–5mm
  ├─────────────────────┤  ← Sub-face (ayous/spruce) 0.8–1.2mm
  ├─────────────────────┤  ← Carbon layer (ALC/ZLC) 0.2–0.3mm
  └─────────────────────┘  ← Face ply (limba/koto) 0.5–0.7mm

Total thickness: 5.5–7.5mm typically
Total weight: 80–95g (blade only, no rubber)
```

### Carbon Placement Effect
- **Outer carbon** (near face): More direct, explosive, less dwell time. Harder to control spin.
- **Inner carbon** (near core): Better feel, longer dwell, more forgiving. "Innerforce" concept.
- Modern trend strongly favors inner carbon (Butterfly Innerforce ALC, Yinhe V14 = inner carbon)

## Weight Considerations

- Light blade (<80g): More spin potential, less power, good for all-wood
- Medium blade (80–90g): Standard, balanced
- Heavy blade (>90g): More power, harder to maneuver, fatiguing

With rubber attached (both sides):
- Light setup: 160–170g total
- Standard: 170–185g
- Heavy: 185–200g

Players are VERY weight-sensitive — advertise blade weight and weight range.

## What Makes a Blade "Premium"

1. **Wood selection**: Hand-picked timber vs random selection
2. **Aging**: Premium wood aged 2–5 years (moisture stability)
3. **Precision**: Thickness tolerance ±0.1mm vs ±0.5mm
4. **Carbon quality**: ALC weave consistency, fiber tension
5. **Finish**: Lacquer coat, handle craftsmanship, logo quality
6. **QC**: Each blade tested for speed/vibration uniformity

The actual MATERIAL cost difference between a $5 blade and a $50 blade is maybe $3–$8. 
The difference is in: selection, precision, QC rejection rate, and brand premium.
