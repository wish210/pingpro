# Table Tennis Equipment Market Landscape

## Market Structure

The table tennis equipment market has 4 tiers of suppliers, each with distinct characteristics:

### Tier 1 — Premium Japanese (Own Factories)
| Brand | HQ | Key Fact |
|-------|-----|----------|
| Butterfly (Tamasu) | Tokyo | Market leader, proprietary tech (ALC, ZLC, Super ZLC), $100–$250 blades |
| Nittaku | Tokyo | Traditional craftsmanship, acoustic wood selection, $80–$200 |
| Victas (formerly TSP) | Tokyo | Newer brand identity, aggressive pro sponsorships, $70–$180 |

- Control full supply chain
- Strictly protect dealer territories and MAP pricing
- Getting authorized dealer status requires volume commitments
- Technology moats: patented carbon weaves (ALC = Arylate-Carbon, ZLC = Zylon-Carbon)

### Tier 2 — European Brands (Mix Own + OEM)
| Brand | HQ | Manufacturing |
|-------|-----|--------------|
| Donic | Germany | Some own, some China OEM |
| Tibhar | Germany | Mix Germany/China |
| Stiga | Sweden | Mix Sweden/China |
| JOOLA | Germany/USA | Mostly China OEM |
| Andro | Germany | Mix |
| XIOM | South Korea | Mix Korea/China |

- Many outsource lower-end models to Chinese OEM factories
- Maintain premium lines in-house or at trusted partner factories
- More open to new dealers than Japanese brands
- JOOLA's growth strategy: aggressive US expansion, pickleball crossover

- Lead time: 2–6 weeks for production runs

## Manufacturing Hubs (China)

1. **Yangzhou, Jiangsu** — blade specialty cluster (Loki Sports HQ here)
2. **Hangzhou, Zhejiang** — general sports equipment manufacturing
3. **Shijiazhuang, Hebei** — Yinhe/Galaxy HQ and factory
4. **Shanghai** — DHS HQ; also Telamon Sports Goods (OEM exporter)
5. **Guangzhou** — Sanwei HQ; southern manufacturing hub

## ITTF Regulations (Constraints)

- At least 85% of blade thickness must be natural wood
- Permitted non-wood layers: carbon fiber, glass fiber, Kevlar, aluminum fiber, cork
- Rubber must be ITTF-approved (separate approval from blade)
- Blade itself does NOT need ITTF approval — only rubber does
- This means: private-label blades face NO certification barrier
